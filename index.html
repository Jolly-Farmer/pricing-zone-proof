Source data review (Excel R3)

ALL ZONES Erysimum Brightside Magenta Glow = $0.10

Perennials, 51-strip. Priced at $0.100 while its sibling Brightside Orange is $0.943 and the average 51-strip perennial is ~$0.92. It's also the only item in the file where levels flatten (L3=L4=L5) and its Zone E/Zone 2 ratio is 7.8× (everything else averages 2.2×). Almost certainly a decimal slip — likely should be ~$0.95–$1.00.

ALL ZONES 256-tray pricing is below the size curve — all 331 items

Every single item sold in both 256 and 288 trays charges less per plant for the 256 than the 288 (e.g. Ageratum Kona: 144 = $0.340, 256 = $0.127, 288 = $0.187, 512 = $0.103). Bigger plugs should cost more per plant, and 288s match their normal curve — only the 256 column is off, sitting just ~17% above 512 bulk pricing. A full 256 tray averages $34.6 vs $50.1 (144) / $54.5 (288) / $60.4 (512). Affects 251 Annuals, 76 Vegetables, 4 Herbs, in every zone and in the printed PDFs. Confirm the 256 rate basis with Samuel.

ZONE 3 Cheaper than Zone 2 across the board

96.5% of all items (and 120/120 combo kits) are ~0.8% cheaper in Zone 3 than Zone 2, so the ladder runs 3 < 2 < 4 < 5 < 6. It's too consistent to be a typo — but confirm the zone rate table is intentional, since the freight sheet charges Zone 3 more per box than Zone 2.

ALL ZONES EOD averages 9.1%, catalog says 10%

The Early Order Discount actually baked into the numbers is 8.4–10%, averaging 9.1% in every zone. The Discounts page promises "billed at 10% off the regular price." If EOD rows are derived from an unrounded base this may be fine — but worth confirming the math is what Samuel intends.

ALL ZONES Discounts-page sample table shows Zone E prices

The miniature example price list on the Discounts Explained page (Ageratum Kona $0.900…) is identical in all 10 PDFs and uses Zone E numbers — the page's hidden slug even says "Changes between every zone." A US Zone 2 grower reading the example sees prices 2.6× their real ones. Confirm whether the sample is meant to be zone-live or generic.

ALL ZONES Cilantro & Cosmos Sensation escalate like no other item

Cilantro (144) and Cosmos Sensation (144) climb +50% from Zone 2→6 (file average +22%) and +59% on the D→E step alone (average +31%). Zone E ends at 3.3× the Zone 2 price vs 2.2× for everything else. They're the only two items on their own rate curve besides the Erysimum error — worth a look.

Erysimum-class suspects (single-item anomalies)

ALL ZONES Lobelia Premium Masterpiece — 26-strip is 2.3× off its own curve

Masterpiece Blue with Eye: 144 = $0.435, 288 = $0.259, but 26-strip = $1.967. Across 379 items the 26/144 ratio medians 1.93 (next-highest is 2.45); this one is 4.52 — totally isolated. The 144 & 288 agree with each other, so either the 26 should be ~$0.84, or the 144/288 should be ~$1.02/$0.62. The 26-strip is also a much newer item number (G037412 vs G004202) — priced separately when it was added.

ALL ZONES Cymbopogon Lemon Grass — 288 is ~50% above its curve

26 = $0.985, 144 = $0.571, 288 = $0.414. Both independent ratios say the 288 should be ~$0.21–$0.28: its 144/288 ratio is 1.38 vs 2.04 median, 26/288 is 2.38 vs 4.60 median. Buying 288s only saves 27% per plant vs 144s when the norm is ~50%. (Fennel Dragon 288 = $0.217 shows the same pattern, milder.)

ALL ZONES Pepper Jalapeno — M vs La Bomba II inconsistent by size

At 144, M ($0.411) is only 11% cheaper than La Bomba II ($0.463). But at 256/288/512 the gap balloons to 29–44% (e.g. 512: $0.127 vs $0.228). La Bomba II's bulk trays sit well above the normal size curve (512/144 = 0.49 vs 0.34 typical) while M is normal — one of the two ladders doesn't follow the house curve.

ALL ZONES Tomato Empress — priciest veg, flattest curve

26-strip at $2.67 (next tomato is ~$1.03) with the shallowest volume curve in Vegetables (L5/L1 = 0.87 vs 0.75 median). High price + flat levels is the same signature the Erysimum error has. If Empress really is a premium grafted line this is fine — but it's worth one look.

ALL ZONES Two liners ~35% above the 51-strip curve

Cuphea Cupheasy Lavender "2024" (51 = $0.877 vs 144 = $0.529) and Evolvulus Hawaiian Blue Eyes (51 = $0.826 vs 144 = $0.52) have 51/144 ratios of 1.66/1.59 where the median is 1.21 — their 51-strips are ~35% above where the curve predicts. The "2024" in the Cuphea name in a 2027 catalog also looks like a stale carry-over.
